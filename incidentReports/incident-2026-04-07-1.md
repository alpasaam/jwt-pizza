# Incident: 2026-04-07

## Summary

On **2026-04-07**, between approximately **13:00 and 15:20 Mountain Time**, authenticated users could not complete **pizza orders**. `POST /api/order` on **JWT Pizza Service** (`pizza-service.saamn.dev`) returned **HTTP 500** with **`Failed to fulfill order at factory`**. The error JSON included **`followLinkToEndChaos`**, a support URL on **JWT Pizza Factory** (`pizza-factory.cs329.click`). Visiting that URL returned `{"message":"Problem resolved. Pizza is back on the menu!"}`, after which purchase failures stopped and successful orders resumed.

This was a **high-severity** incident: **order placement was unavailable** for production users for roughly **two hours** of visible impact (failure activity in metrics from **13:06** through **15:16**). The event was detected by a **Grafana alert** at **13:06**. **Saam Naeini** (on-call) began active investigation at **15:10** and mitigated by following the Factory recovery link. The underlying issue was **not** defective JWT Pizza Service application code; the **Factory fulfillment API** rejected orders until chaos was cleared via the vendor-provided URL.

After recovery, **Grafana alert thresholds were tuned** because synthetic traffic (including **too-many-items** order attempts) inflated **pizza failure** metrics and obscured Factory-only outages. Details are documented under **Alert rule updates**.

## Detection

This incident was detected when a **Grafana alert** fired at **13:06** (Mountain Time) on elevated **purchase failure** and related signals.

**Saam Naeini** confirmed the issue by reviewing the **Pizza purchases** dashboard (**purchase failures** spiking with traffic) and **Loki** logs: repeated **`POST /api/order`** with **status 500**, **`authorized: true`**, valid request bodies, and response bodies containing **`Failed to fulfill order at factory`** and **`followLinkToEndChaos`**.

Time-to-triage could be improved: roughly **two hours** passed between the first alert (**13:06**) and opening Grafana (**15:10**). Faster **OnCall acknowledgement**, a clearer **dashboard** distinguishing Factory chaos from synthetic failures, or **alert routing** that prioritizes Factory-related symptoms would cut that delay.

## Impact

For approximately **two hours** between **13:06** and **15:18** Mountain Time on **04/07/2026**, users experienced **failed checkout**: **`POST /api/order`** returned **500** and orders could not be fulfilled.

**JWT Pizza Service** (`pizza-service.saamn.dev`) returned errors because calls to **JWT Pizza Factory** (`pizza-factory.cs329.click`) did not succeed until chaos was cleared. Evidence included Grafana **pizza purchases / purchase failures** time series and Loki HTTP rows with full request/response context.

## Timeline

All times are **Mountain Time**.

- _13:06_ — Grafana alert fires; failure spikes appear in metrics.
- _13:06–15:09_ — Ongoing order failures; synthetic traffic continues.
- _15:10_ — **Saam Naeini** opens Grafana; reviews **Pizza purchases** and **Loki**.
- _15:12_ — Root cause identified: Factory fulfillment failure; **`followLinkToEndChaos`** in log response indicates recovery path.
- _15:16_ — **Saam Naeini** opens **`followLinkToEndChaos`**; Factory returns **“Problem resolved. Pizza is back on the menu!”**; failure spikes stop.
- _15:18_ — Healthy state confirmed: successful **pizza purchases** in metrics without paired failure spikes.

## Response

**Saam Naeini** responded as on-call. After the **13:06** alert, active debugging did not begin until **15:10**. Once engaged, **Saam Naeini** opened the **Pizza purchases** panel, correlated **purchase failures** with **Loki** **`POST /api/order`** **500** entries, and used **`Failed to fulfill order at factory`** and **`followLinkToEndChaos`** to determine remediation. From the start of investigation to mitigation was on the order of **minutes**.

## Root cause

**JWT Pizza Factory** did not fulfill orders during the chaos window. JWT Pizza Service proxied orders correctly; when the Factory rejected fulfillment, the service returned **500** with the documented error payload and recovery link. The **immediate** cause was **Factory-side chaos / fulfillment rejection**, not invalid authentication or malformed requests (logs show **`authorized: true`** and expected JSON).

## Resolution

**Saam Naeini** opened **`followLinkToEndChaos`** from the error JSON (Factory “end chaos” support endpoint) and verified the response: **`Problem resolved. Pizza is back on the menu!`** In Grafana, **purchase failure** spikes ended around **15:16** and successful **pizza purchases** appeared by **15:18**, confirming recovery.

## Prevention

Synthetic load should stay **realistic and lower-rate** so **pizza failure** alerts reflect **operational** Factory problems rather than expected test failures. A short **runbook** entry for **`Failed to fulfill order at factory`** should point engineers to **`followLinkToEndChaos`** and Grafana verification. **Known chaos windows** should come with an expectation that on-call **acknowledges alerts within minutes**.

## Action items

1. **Saam Naeini** refined Grafana alert rules (thresholds and/or query windows) after **2026-04-07** to reduce false positives from synthetic **too-many-items** and similar traffic while keeping sensitivity to Factory chaos; tracking is the Grafana Cloud alert rule history.

1. **Saam Naeini** kept the traffic generator conservative (throttled `generatePizzaTraffic.sh` with an 8-hour auto-stop) to limit cost and alert noise; changes live in **jwt-pizza-service** `scripts/generatePizzaTraffic.sh`.

1. **Saam Naeini** will acknowledge OnCall pages within minutes on future scheduled chaos test days so triage starts closer to the first alert.

## Alert rule updates

The **traffic generator** produced **pizza failures** (including **too-many-items** paths), inflating **`pizza_failures`** / related series and making it harder to tell **Factory chaos** from **self-induced** load.

**Saam Naeini** adjusted Grafana alert **thresholds** and/or **evaluation windows** after the incident to cut noise from synthetic traffic while preserving detection of sustained Factory-related failures seen on **2026-04-07**. The authoritative definitions are in Grafana Cloud under **Alerting → Alert rules** for the JWT Pizza / pizza-failure rules in this stack.
